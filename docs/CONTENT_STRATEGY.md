# LinkedIn Lead Gen SaaS - Content Strategy

## 📋 Executive Summary

This content strategy outlines how we'll attract, engage, and convert B2B professionals through valuable, educational content that positions LeadFlow AI as the go-to solution for intelligent LinkedIn lead generation.

---

## 🎯 Content Objectives

### Primary Goals
1. **Drive Awareness**: Reach 10k+ B2B professionals in target segments
2. **Generate Leads**: Convert 5% of engaged audience to trials
3. **Establish Authority**: Become top-of-mind for LinkedIn lead gen
4. **Enable Sales**: Provide resources that shorten sales cycle
5. **Retain Customers**: Keep users engaged and reduce churn

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Organic traffic: +200% YoY
- Email subscribers: 5,000 in Q1
- Trial signups: 500/month
- Content engagement rate: >8%
- Customer retention: >90%

## 🎬 Content Formats & Distribution

### Written Content

#### Long-form Blog Posts (Pillar Content)
**Characteristics:**
- 2000-3000 words
- Comprehensive, definitive guides
- SEO optimized with keyword clusters
- Rich with examples, screenshots, data

**Distribution:**
- Company blog (primary)
- LinkedIn article (republished after 2 weeks)
- Newsletter feature
- Social media snippets

**Example Template:**
```
Title: [How to/Ultimate Guide to] [Topic]
Introduction (problem statement)
Section 1: Current state analysis
Section 2: Framework/methodology
Section 3: Step-by-step implementation
Section 4: Common mistakes to avoid
Section 5: Advanced tips
Conclusion + CTA
```

---

#### LinkedIn Native Posts

**Carousel Posts** (High Engagement)
- 6-10 slides
- Visual storytelling
- Swipeable insights
- Strong hook on slide 1

**Templates:**
- "5 Mistakes You're Making on LinkedIn"
- "The Anatomy of a Perfect Cold Message"
- "Before & After: Profile Transformation"

**Text Posts** (Thought Leadership)
- Personal stories with business lessons
- Contrarian takes (backed by data)
- Framework/models you use

**Hook formulas:**
- "Everyone thinks X. But here's why Y..."
- "I made a $100K mistake so you don't have to..."
- "After analyzing 1000 [X], here's what I learned..."

## 🎨 Content Creation Workflow

### Ideation Phase
**Weekly brainstorm session:**
- Review trending topics (Google Trends, LinkedIn)
- Analyze competitor content
- Survey customer questions (support tickets, sales calls)
- Check keyword opportunities
- Review content calendar gaps

**Idea Sources:**
- Customer conversations
- Support tickets/FAQs
- Sales objections
- Industry news
- Data/analytics insights
- Personal experiences

---

### Production Phase

**Blog Post Workflow:**
1. **Outline** (30 min) - Structure with H2/H3
2. **First draft** (2-3 hours) - Write without editing
3. **Research & data** (1 hour) - Add stats, examples, screenshots
4. **Edit** (1 hour) - Clarity, flow, conce
5. **SEO optimization** (30 min) - Keywords, meta, links
6. **Design** (1 hour) - Featured image, in-post graphics
7. **Review** (30 min) - Final proofread
8. **Publish & promote** (1 hour) - Schedule, social posts

**Total time**: ~8 hours per pillar post

---

### Review & Approval Process

**Level 1**: Self-edit (writer)
**Level 2**: Peer review (another content creator)
**Level 3**: Expert review (subject matter expert for technical accuracy)
**Level 4**: Final approval (Content Lead)

**Checklist before publishing:**
- [ ] Brand voice consistent
- [ ] Grammar/spelling checked (Grammarly)
- [ ] Facts verified
- [ ] Links working
- [ ] Images optimized
- [ ] Mobile preview checked
- [ ] CTA clear and compelling

## ✅ Content Quality Standards

### Every Piece of Content Must:
1. **Solve a real problem** for target audience
2. **Provide actionable takeaways** (not just theory)
3. **Reflect brand voice** (intelligent, empowering, professional)
4. **Meet quality bar** (no typos, factual errors)
5. **Include clear CTA** (what should reader do next?)
6. **Be visually appealing** (proper formatting, images)
7. **Be accessible** (readable for non-experts)
8. **Add unique value** (original insight, not rehashed content)
